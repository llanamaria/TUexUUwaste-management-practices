What this is about

Why sorting matters and what both campuses are already doing

Both TU/e and UU have been actively cutting waste at the source. Here is what is already in place, and where you come in.

Shared goal

Every correctly sorted item is a win

When waste is separated properly it can be reused or recycled, turning it back into raw material instead of burning it. Each kilogram that avoids incineration saves 840 grams of CO₂. Both universities have committed to zero waste by 2030, and every person on campus plays a role.

TU/e progress

Eindhoven campus

  • Tulip collection stations installed across all buildings
  • PMD stream introduced alongside old plastic stream
  • Single-use plastics banned on campus
  • Billie Cup replaces disposable cups campus-wide
  • Chain cooperation with Asito, Vermaat & Appèl: 120 kg of plastic saved per year
UU progress

Utrecht campus

  • Scheid Zooi campaign launched to educate staff & students
  • Dedicated lab-plastic stream introduced in faculties
  • Materials transit point for reuse of furniture & equipment
  • Circular procurement guidelines for suppliers
  • Waste scans conducted to identify and reduce residual streams
Campus-specific rules

What goes where

The four main streams are shared by both universities, but specific items are sorted differently. Select your campus below to see the rules that apply to you.

🟠 Plastic & Carton (PMD)

Must be empty
  • Empty PET / drink bottle
  • Empty drink carton
  • Empty snack package
  • Foam, aluminium → Other waste
  • Mixed packages → Other waste

🟢 Organic

Food & natural waste
  • Food leftovers
  • Fruit scraps & peels
  • Tea bags
  • Coffee grounds
  • Food packaging → PMD bin
  • Bio-plastics → Other waste

⚫ Other Waste

Residual
  • Dirty paper & cardboard
  • Wooden cutlery
  • Cans, bags of chips
  • Tissues (used or clean)
  • Bio-plastics
  • Aluminium foil
  • Glass → Separate container
  • Batteries → Separate point

🔵 Paper

Dry only
  • Printing paper
  • Newspaper
  • Window envelope
  • Cardboard (clean & dry)
  • Pizza box → Other waste
  • Greasy paper → Other waste
🌷 Tulip stations: Individual desk bins have been replaced. Look for a Tulip collection point, one per 25 workplaces. Glass and batteries have dedicated separate containers on campus.

🟠 Plastic

Soft & film plastic
  • Plastic drink bottle (non-deposit)
  • Transparent plastic food packaging
  • Plastic bags & sandwich bags
  • Chips bag / snack wrapper
  • Transparent or white plastic cup
  • Plastic food container
  • Hard plastic → Residual waste
  • Mixed paper/plastic → Residual

🟢 GFT (Organic)

Food, garden & organic
  • Fruit & vegetable scraps
  • Tea bags & coffee grounds
  • Bread, pasta, cooked food
  • Nuts, eggshells
  • Plants & leaves
  • Compostable cutlery → Residual
  • Paper straw → Residual

⚫ Residual Waste

Everything else
  • Used / dirty tissue
  • Wooden or compostable cutlery
  • Aluminium foil & lids
  • Mixed-material packaging
  • Hard plastic
  • Greasy pizza box

🔵 Paper

Dry & clean
  • Printing / office paper
  • Newspaper & magazine
  • Cardboard & envelopes
  • Clean (unused) tissue
  • Clean pizza box
  • Clean paper coffee filter
  • Greasy or dirty paper → Residual
♻️ UU reminder: At Utrecht, chips bags go in plastic and a clean pizza box goes in paper two of the most common sorting mistakes made by people coming from TU/e.

Eindhoven University of Technology

TU/e: Waste management in depth

Everything you need to know about how waste is handled across the TU/e campus.

🌷

Tulip Collection Stations

Individual desk bins have been removed and replaced by central Tulip stations, one per 25 workplaces. Each has clearly labelled openings for PMD, organic, other waste and paper.

♻️

PMD Stream

TU/e introduced the PMD stream (Plastic, Metal & Drink cartons) with the help of Renewi, replacing the older plastic-only collection. All emptied plastic bottles and drink cartons go here.

☕

The Billie Cup

Disposable coffee cups are no longer available on campus. The reusable Billie Cup has replaced them at TU/e coffee machines. Return it to the designated collection point rather than any bin.

🚫

No Single-Use Plastics

Single-use plastic items are banned across the entire TU/e campus. Facilities provide homogeneous packaging materials to make proper sorting easier at the point of disposal.

🍽️

Food Waste Reduction

The Atlas restaurant has joined "Too Good To Go" so surplus food finds a home. Caterer Vermaat adapted their menus and banqueting folder to reduce preparation waste.

⚗️

Hazardous Waste

TU/e's Chemical Distribution Center and Recycling Center handle all chemical and hazardous waste. Hazardous waste is transported daily from building depots to the CDC for safe disposal.

Utrecht University

UU: Waste management in depth

How Utrecht University approaches zero-waste operations across its campus and faculties.

🗂️

Scheid Zooi Campaign

UU's "Scheid Zooi" (Separate Your Stuff) campaign raises awareness among students, staff and visitors. It provides clear sorting guidance and an online tool to look up where any item belongs.

🧪

Lab Plastic Collection

UU has introduced a dedicated stream for lab-use plastic so it gets a second life rather than being incinerated. Look for the lab-plastic collection point in your faculty building.

🪑

Materials Reuse Point

A transit point for materials allows furniture, office equipment and other items to be passed on for reuse rather than discarded, supporting the university's circular procurement goals.

🛒

Circular Procurement

UU works with suppliers to ensure purchased goods are designed for a longer life and easier recycling. Packaging requirements are built into supplier agreements.

📊

Waste Scans

Regular waste scans assess what is being thrown away and where. Results inform targeted campaigns and infrastructure changes to reduce the amount of residual waste produced.

🤝

Student Collaboration

UU actively works with students to gain insight into campus waste patterns. Student projects feed directly into the zero-waste programme's next steps and policy decisions.

Good habits

Practical tips, sorted by campus

What applies everywhere, what is specific to Eindhoven, and what is specific to Utrecht.

♻️ Both universities
🫙

Empty before you sort

Plastic bottles and drink cartons must be rinsed and empty before going in the plastic/PMD bin. Liquid residue contaminates the entire recycling stream and renders it unusable.

🌿

Bio-plastics ≠ organic

"Compostable" or bio-plastic packaging goes to residual waste at both universities. Campus composting systems cannot process it. Look at the actual material, not the marketing label.

🍕

Greasy cardboard is residual

Once a box or wrapper is greasy it cannot be recycled as paper at either university. When in doubt about cleanliness, it goes in residual waste. Always the safer choice.

🔋

Batteries always go separate

Batteries, e-cigarettes and small electronics should never enter any of the four main bins. Both campuses have dedicated collection points. Find yours before you need to dispose of one.

TU/e specific Eindhoven
🌷

Find your Tulip

Your desk bin is gone. Tulip central collection stations are placed throughout every TU/e building, one per 25 workplaces. Take the extra steps; the sorting infrastructure is there for a reason.

☕

Use and return the Billie Cup

Disposable cups are no longer on campus. Borrow a Billie Cup from the coffee machine and return it to the designated collection point, not the bin, when you are finished.

🛍️

Chips bags → Other Waste at TU/e

At TU/e, snack wrappers and chips bags go in Other Waste, not the PMD bin. This differs from UU. Keep it in mind if you study or work at both campuses.

🍽️

Too Good To Go at Atlas

The Atlas restaurant uses "Too Good To Go" to prevent food waste at the end of each day. Download the app and grab surplus meals at a reduced price rather than seeing good food discarded.

UU specific Utrecht
🛍️

Chips bags → Plastic at UU

At UU, snack wrappers and chips bags go in the plastic bin, unlike TU/e where they are Other Waste. One of the most common sorting mix-ups between the two campuses.

🧻

Clean tissue → Paper at UU

An unused, clean tissue goes in the paper bin at Utrecht. At TU/e all tissues go in Other Waste regardless. The rule at UU only changes once a tissue has actually been used. Dirty ones are always residual.

🍕

Clean pizza box → Paper at UU

A completely clean pizza box goes in the paper bin at UU. At TU/e it is Other Waste. If there is any grease at all, it is residual waste at both universities.

🧪

Lab plastic has its own stream

If you work in a UU lab, look for the dedicated lab-plastic collection in your faculty building. These plastics should not go in the general plastic bin. They need a separate stream.
